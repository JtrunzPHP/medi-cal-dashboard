What You Are Looking At

This platform tracks Medi-Cal managed care enrollment across all 58 CA counties and maps them to publicly traded MCOs. Enrollment changes directly impact revenue — member losses = premium headwinds before the market prices them in.
  • Centene (CNC): Health Net + CA Health & Wellness + CalViva + CHPIV subcontracts. ~1.5M CA members.
  • Molina (MOH): Direct contracts + LA subcontract (~50% of LA Health Net). ~1.2M CA target.
  • Elevance (ELV): Anthem Blue Cross — exited 14+ counties in 2024. Intentionally smaller footprint.
  • Kaiser (KP): Private — tracked for competitive context. Expanded to 32 counties in 2024.
  • UNH: D-SNP only in CA, no direct Medi-Cal managed care contract.
How to use: Compare DHCS actuals to management guidance. If DHCS shows accelerating member losses vs. what management guided, that's a potential earnings miss signal. The global toggle (top-right) adjusts MOH/CNC for the LA subcontract across all tabs.

Investment Signals

Membership trajectory is the single most important leading indicator for Medicaid MCO earnings. DHCS data updates monthly; sell-side models update quarterly. This gives you a ~3 month data advantage.
  • MoM Growth Rate: Month-over-month enrollment change %. Sustained negative = revenue headwind. When MoM turns positive after a decline, that's an inflection point — often precedes a stock re-rating.
  • YoY Growth Rate: Smooths seasonality. If YoY is bending upward (less negative), the membership floor is forming.
  • 3-Month Momentum: Smoothed MoM for trend identification. Crossing from negative to positive = buy signal. Crossing from positive to negative = sell signal.
  • 6-Month Velocity: Medium-term trend direction. Captures structural shifts vs. noise.
  • Guidance Overlay: Enter management's stated CA membership target below. A dashed line appears on the Cumulative chart. If actuals are below the dashed line, the company is tracking below guidance — potential earnings miss.
  • Cumulative from Peak: Shows members lost since each MCO's historical peak. The gap = revenue that has evaporated. Dashed line = management target. Convergence toward the line = recovery.
  • Redetermination Cycle (48mo): The full pandemic enrollment story — surge from continuous enrollment → cliff from PHE unwinding → stabilization. The shape of this curve tells you where we are in the cycle.
⚠️ Nov/Dec 2024 Note: Kaiser auto-assignment phased in across 32 counties starting July 2024. By Q4 2024 significant member flows to Kaiser may distort other MCOs' share metrics. Year-end redetermination batch processing may also contribute. Interpret Q4 2024 shifts with caution.

Language as Immigration Proxy

Primary language of newly Medi-Cal eligible individuals.
  • Why it matters: Non-English enrollment growth signals immigration-driven demand. If non-English share is climbing, that means demographic-driven enrollment is growing faster than domestic — a tailwind for MCOs in high-immigration counties regardless of redetermination headwinds.
  • Non-English Share %: The key signal. Rising = immigration-driven growth accelerating.
  • County threshold data: Shows non-English languages meeting DHCS thresholds (3,000 beneficiaries or 5%). English is estimated as County Total minus sum of threshold languages.

Risk & Analytics

  • Displacement Tracker: Who gained/lost members from the 2024 restructuring. If CNC lost 50K in a county and KP gained 50K, you know where those members went.
  • Growth Heatmap: Color-coded MoM growth by county per MCO. Red clusters = geographic-specific problems (rate cuts? provider exits?).
  • Share Shift: Who is gaining/losing statewide market share. Share gainers in a declining market = outperformers.
  • HHI: Geographic concentration risk. >2500 = high risk. One county policy change can wipe out a significant book.
  • Exit/Entry Timeline: Which plans left/entered counties — shows competitive dynamics in real time.
  • Redetermination Severity: Counties with the largest peak-to-current decline — shows where the unwinding hit hardest.
